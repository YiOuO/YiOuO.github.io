Yi Zhan

PhD Student
Ocean College, Zhejiang University

I’m a final year PhD student in Marine Technology and Engineering at Zhejiang University under the supervision of Prof. Min Luo and Prof. Abbas Khayyer. I’m currently a visiting scholar at the EPhysLab in Spain, under the supervision of Prof. Alejandro J. C. Crespo.

My research interests lie at the intersection of computational fluid dynamics, high performance computing, and artificial intelligence. Specifically, I focus on:

  • Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) methods for fluid-structure interaction problems
  • High Performance Computing (HPC) with GPU parallelization for large-scale simulations
  • Reinforcement Learning in Computational Fluid Dynamics (RLCFD) for intelligent flow control

selected publications

  1. Coupling Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics with Multi-Agent Deep Reinforcement Learning (MADRL) for Cooperative Control of Point Absorbers
    Yi Zhan, Iván Martínez-Estévez, Min Luo, and 2 more authors
    arXiv preprint arXiv:2601.06485, 2026
    Abs HTML

    This study develops an optimal adaptive damping control model for PTO systems by coupling high-fidelity SPH simulations with Multi-Agent Soft Actor-Critic (MASAC) to maximize wave energy capture.

  2. DualSPHysics+: An Enhanced DualSPHysics with Improvements in Accuracy, Energy Conservation and Resolution of the Continuity Equation
    Yi Zhan, Min Luo, and Abbas Khayyer
    Computer Physics Communications, 2025
    Abs HTML PDF Code

    This paper presents an enhanced version of the well-known SPH open-source code DualSPHysics for the simulation of free-surface fluid flows. The enhancements include Optimized Particle Shifting, Riemann stabilization, and improved volume conservation schemes.

  3. An Enhanced SPH-Based Hydroelastic FSI Solver with Structural Dynamic Hourglass Control
    Yi Zhan, Min Luo, and Abbas Khayyer
    Journal of Fluids and Structures, 2025
    Abs HTML

    An enhanced hydroelastic FSI solver with second-order discretization, Riemann stabilization, and a novel Dynamic Hourglass Control scheme for suppressing zero-energy modes.

  4. An Enhanced Numerical Wave Tank for Wave-Structure Interaction Based on DualSPHysics+
    Yi Zhan, Min Luo, and Abbas Khayyer
    Ocean Engineering, 2025
    Abs HTML

    An enhanced numerical wave tank for wave-structure interaction simulations incorporating advanced schemes for wave generation, propagation, and structure interaction with improved accuracy and physical consistency.
